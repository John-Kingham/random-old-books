Why read random old books?

Reading random old books is a journey into the unexpected—a portal to forgotten worlds, lost wisdom, and voices that once shaped history.

Picking up an obscure, timeworn volume isn't just reading—it's an act of exploration, a rebellion against the limits of your comfort zone. If you want discovery, mystery, and the thrill of the unknown, crack open a random old book. You may never see the world the same way again.

They will surprise you

Reading random book is like stepping through an unseen door—you never know what awaits. The words on the page refuse to conform to expectations, pulling you into a world stranger, deeper and more surprising than you imagined.

They will challenge you

Reading old books is a test of patience and perspective. The language feels unfamiliar, ideas clash with modern sensibilities, and cultural contexts demand decoding. But that challenge sharpens the mind—forcing you to wrestle with history, question assumptions, and navigate forgotten ways of thinking.

They will expand your mind

Random old books can unlocking hidden doors to knowledge you never knew you needed. They broaden your understanding and, in many cases, the more unexpected the book, the more you learn.

Visit our bookshop

At our bookshop in Book Town, every visit is an adventure, with bookshelves lined with random old books, waiting to surprise and inspire you. We don't organise our books by genre or title, so you can experience the thrill of discovery, the joy of serendipity, and the chance to stumble upon a story you never knew you needed.

Come to our events

We regularly invite authors to share their experiences at intimate, engaging events in our bookshop. Over an hour or so, they talk about the struggles and triumphs behind their writing, offering a glimpse into the creative process. Whether you're an avid reader or an aspiring writer, these sessions promise fresh perspectives and fascinating stories.
